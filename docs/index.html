      How these prices work

      What the number is

      Every price on this site is the TCGplayer Market Price — TCGplayer's smoothed average of what copies actually sold for on its marketplace (Near Mint, raw/ungraded) — captured once a day (~20:00 UTC) via tcgcsv.com. Each card is priced by its most valuable regular printing (shown in the card view, e.g. Holofoil). 1st Edition printings are excluded: they rarely trade on TCGplayer, so their "market" figures there are unreliable. If a card has no recent sales data, its last-known price is carried forward for up to 70 days (marked † in the table) rather than showing a false crash to $0.

      Rarely, TCGplayer prints an obviously wrong market price for a thinly traded card (a $2,000 card flashing $500 for a day). A price far outside a card's own recent range is held at its recent median until later data confirms it, so a source glitch can't move the index. And because a card's daily change is only meaningful between two confirmed prices, cards without one on both days show "—" instead of a percentage and sit out of the movers list — vintage cards that trade a few times a year don't have a reliable day-to-day price, and we'd rather show nothing than a made-up number.

      Why other sites show different prices

      There is no single "true" price for a trading card — every tracker measures a different market. PriceCharting averages eBay sold listings (auctions and all conditions mixed), so it often reads lower than TCGplayer for high-end cards. eBay search results are mostly asking prices, which run higher than what sales actually close at. Graded (PSA/BGS/CGC) prices are a different market entirely and can be several times the raw price. The gaps are biggest on rare vintage cards that sell only a few times a year — two trackers can legitimately differ by 2× on the same card. This index uses one consistent, sales-based yardstick (TCGplayer Market) across all 500 cards, which is what an index needs; use the compare links in each card's detail view to see the same card on TCGplayer, PriceCharting, and eBay.

      The S&Poké 500 is a price-weighted index of the 500 most valuable English, raw (ungraded) Pokémon trading cards, rebased to 1,000 at launch. It moves with the combined market price of the basket — up when cards get pricier, down when the market cools. Prices are TCGplayer market data via tcgcsv.com, refreshed daily. Each card is priced by its regular printing (1st Edition printings are out of scope — they trade too thinly on TCGplayer for reliable daily pricing). Other price trackers measure different markets and will show different numbers — see how these prices work.
